# Shadow Apps

Shadow Apps are external-facing apps, previews, internal tools, demos, or websites that exist outside the official asset inventory.

They often appear because teams can now build and deploy quickly with AI tools and modern platforms.

## Why Shadow Apps matter

These assets can expose real risk even when they were never part of a formal security process.

Common examples include:

- Preview deployments
- Marketing microsites
- Internal tools exposed to the internet
- Demo apps built for prospects or pilots
- Fast-moving side projects tied to production data or credentials

## What CyBox helps you do

CyBox helps teams:

- Discover internet-facing assets
- Review posture at the asset level
- Understand which assets need attention first
- Link live exposure back to the broader security workflow

## What to look for

A useful Shadow Apps review usually starts with:

- Which assets are public?
- Which assets have serious posture issues?
- Which assets look abandoned or unmanaged?
- Which assets should be scanned more closely with live exposure validation?

## Suggested workflow

1. Connect supported deployment or domain sources
2. Review newly discovered assets
3. Open the highest-risk assets first
4. Run or review live scans where relevant
5. Decide whether to fix, monitor, or retire the asset

## In the product

A Shadow Apps detail view is most useful when it answers these questions clearly:

- What is this asset?
- Why is it risky?
- What evidence supports that?
- What should the team do next?
