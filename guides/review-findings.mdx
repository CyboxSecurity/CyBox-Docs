## Opening the Findings Page

Click **Findings** in the left sidebar. You'll see all open findings across your organization, sorted by severity by default.

---

## Recommended Triage Order

<Steps>
  <Step title="Filter to Critical and High">
    Use the **Severity** filter to show only Critical and High findings. These are your immediate priorities.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Secrets findings first">
    Filter by **Scanner: Secrets**. Exposed credentials should be rotated immediately — before any other fix work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review SCA findings">
    Filter by **Scanner: SCA**. Known CVEs in dependencies are often the fastest wins — AutoFix can update the version in one click.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Address SAST and IaC findings">
    Work through SAST and IaC findings by asset, starting with your most critical repositories.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Finding Detail View

Click any finding to open the detail panel. It shows:

- **Full description** of the vulnerability
- **Exact location** — file, line number, or URL endpoint
- **Code snippet** (for SAST findings)
- **Remediation guidance** — what to change and why
- **AutoFix button** — if a fix PR can be generated automatically
- **Ignore option** — to dismiss with a reason

---

## Ignoring Findings

If a finding is a false positive or the risk is accepted:

1. Open the finding detail
2. Click **Ignore**
3. Select a reason:
   - **False Positive** — CyBox incorrectly flagged this
   - **Risk Accepted** — known risk, accepted by your team
   - **Not Applicable** — not relevant to your context
4. Add an optional note for your team

Ignored findings are tracked and visible with their reason. They can be reinstated at any time.

---

## Tracking Progress

The **Dashboard** shows your Security Risk Score and finding counts over time. Use it to track whether your posture is improving sprint over sprint.
