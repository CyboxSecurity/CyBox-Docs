## Overview

Assets are the units of work in CyBox. Everything CyBox scans, monitors, and reports on is tied to an asset. Go to **Assets** in the sidebar to manage your full inventory.

---

## Asset types

| Type | What it covers |
|------|----------------|
| **Repository** | A connected GitHub repository. CyBox scans the code, dependencies, credentials, and infrastructure definitions it contains. |
| **Website** | A public URL or domain added manually. CyBox tests it for live exposure. |
| **Service** | A deployed service discovered via a connected platform such as Render or Vercel. |
| **Project** | A project-level deployment unit from a connected platform. |

---

## Asset status

Each asset shows a status that reflects its current scan state:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Active** | Scanning is enabled and the asset is included in scheduled scans |
| **Inactive** | The asset exists in inventory but is not being actively scanned |
| **Never scanned** | The asset has been added but no scan has run yet |

Use the toggle on each asset row to enable or disable active scanning.

---

## Adding an asset

1. Go to **Assets**
2. Click **+ Add Asset**
3. Choose the asset type — Repository or Website
4. For repositories: select from your connected GitHub installation
5. For websites: enter the public URL
6. Save — CyBox will queue an initial scan automatically

---

## Asset sources

Assets can come from two places:

- **Manual** — added directly by your team
- **Auto-discovered** — pulled in from a connected deployment platform such as Vercel or Render

Auto-discovered assets appear with their source platform tagged. They update automatically as your connected platforms sync.

---

## Managing assets

From the Assets list you can:

- **Search** by name
- **Filter** by type — Repos, Websites, or Active only
- **Sort** by name, type, status, last scan date, or active state
- **Trigger a scan** — open an asset and click **Scan Now**
- **Deactivate** — toggle off assets you want to keep in inventory without scanning

---

## Good practice

Start with your most business-critical assets. A focused inventory of five well-monitored assets is more useful than a large inventory with scattered attention. Expand as your team builds confidence in the workflow.
